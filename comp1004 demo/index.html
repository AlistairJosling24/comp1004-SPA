The website which allows you to seemlessely plan your day around your most important tasks whilst also allowing you to stay healthy! Please scroll to navigate the various screens or skip straight to making your plan by using either of the navigation bars available to you.

About Us

VitalPlan is a single page easy access application centered around allowing users to easily plan their daily activities. Our plan alteration algorithm is backed by scientififc research and tailored to the users age , gender and overall daily priorities. After each plan is created you will have the opportunity to modify it to better suit your needs. However you will be provided with feedback on each plan made, advising you on what changes to make to your routine to benefit your health. Please take into concideration the feedback you recieve as it is all based on reasearch and is focused your wellbeing. Enjoy!

Research

Once you have inputted your daily activities, you will be advised on what you can do to possibly make your routine more physically and mentally healthy. As you will be provided with advice, appropriate research has been undertaken to ensure that this advice is as accurate as possible. All recomendations follow the Uk Government guidlines

Exercise

Children age 5 - 18 require a minimum of 60 minutes of exercise per day to stay healthy. For an activity to count as exercise it must make you breath faster and feel warmer Adults 19 or older require require 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, meaning that they require 30 minutes a day monday - friday Exercising in adults can type 2 diabetes by up to 40%, cardiovascular disease by up to 35% ,falls and depression by up to 30% , joint and back pain by up to 25% ,cancers (colon and breast) by up to 20%/p3>

Work

The british heart foundation has classified long working hours as anything over 55 hours meaning roughly 11 hours a day moday - friday , with the average work hours being 35 - 40 per week. Children aged 5-7 require 2 - 3 hours of fucussed learning a day. With that number climbing to 3 - 4 hours of focuissed learning for children aged 8 - 11 Children aged 12 - 14 require 4 - 5 hours including work done at home and finally children aged 15 - 17 require 5 - 7 hours of focussed learning.
